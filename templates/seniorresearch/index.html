Description

 Automated area mapping has been a subject of intense robotic research for years, given its real-world applications in problems such as robotic search-and-rescue and exploration of areas unsafe for humans. Currently, much research has been conducted on 2D and 3D area mapping in single robot systems, but one area specifically that has not been researched much is the use of multiple robots in the automated 3D mapping problem - a swarm robotics approach.

 For my project, I will create three small, mobile swarm robots and equip them with stereo vision cameras, with Raspberry Pis serving as the secondary control system and managing and processing the output from the cameras. Communication with the Raspberry Pis will be established using a local area network (LAN) created from a router. A central processing system will aggregate all the data from the Raspberry Pis. Ultimately, the goal is to create software that will efficiently navigate the robots through an area and map a 3D field representation of the area.
